# Problem 1 - Investigating the Range as a Function of the Angle of Projection

## 🎯 Motivation

Projectile motion offers a rich environment to explore kinematic equations and observe how multiple parameters interact. In this task, we aim to understand how the range of a projectile depends on its launch angle.

By visualizing the output of equations under varying launch angles, students gain insight into physical principles in a hands-on, intuitive way.

---

## 📌 Task

### 1. **Theoretical Foundation**:
- Derive the equation for projectile range:  
  $$ R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g} $$  
  where \( v_0 \) is initial velocity, \( \theta \) is angle, and \( g \) is gravitational acceleration.

### 2. **Simulation**:
- Use Python to simulate projectile motion for various angles \( \theta \in [0^\circ, 90^\circ] \)
- Compute and plot the range vs angle curve

### 3. **Analysis**:
- Identify the angle at which maximum range is achieved  
- Discuss the symmetry of the sine function and physical meaning

### 4. **Visualization**:
- Plot: Angle (degrees) vs. Range (meters)

---

## 📦 Deliverables

- `projectile_simulation.py`: Python script for simulation and plotting  
- Graphical output showing range as a function of angle  
- This `.md` file explaining method and interpretation of results  

---

## 🔍 Result Preview

- Maximum range occurs at \( \theta = 45^\circ \)  
- Symmetrical range for \( \theta \) and \( 90^\circ - \theta \)
